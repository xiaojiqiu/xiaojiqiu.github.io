Beyond Datafied Labor: The Algorithmic Dilemmas and Resistance of Food Delivery Riders

By David Qiu

1. Introduction

In China’s booming platform economy, millions of food delivery riders navigate urban streets daily under algorithmic dispatch systems. Platforms like Meituan and Ele.me have rapidly transformed service delivery and labor organization...

2. Literature Review

2.1 Algorithmic Governance and Digital Labor

Management through algorithms has become central to the gig economy. In food delivery, software assigns tasks, monitors riders, and disciplines via automated systems...

2.2 Operations Research and Delivery Algorithms

Delivery platforms use optimization algorithms based on the Vehicle Routing Problem (VRP). These models aim to minimize delivery time and cost while handling real-time constraints...

3. Data and Methodology

This study uses mixed methods: surveys (n=83), ethnographic observation, and document analysis in Shenzhen. We gathered rider perspectives on algorithmic control, work intensity, and safety...

4. Empirical Results

4.1 Algorithmic Control, Efficiency, and Labor Precarity

While algorithmic dispatch increases efficiency, it also increases stress and danger. Over half of riders admit to traffic violations due to time pressure. Gamification exacerbates competition and burnout...

4.2 Riders’ Counter-Mapping and Algorithmic Resistance

Riders have developed hand-drawn “mutual aid maps” to bypass algorithmic blind spots. These counter-maps reflect lived urban knowledge and represent a form of resistance and community building...

5. Discussion

5.1 Integrating Local Knowledge

We propose “experience-informed optimization,” where rider feedback dynamically adjusts routing algorithms. This participatory approach can improve performance and labor trust...

5.2 Policy Implications and Worker Empowerment

  • Algorithm Transparency: Platforms should explain order and rating systems.
  • Rider Autonomy: Let riders choose orders and routes based on real-world knowledge.
  • Labor Protections: Enforce minimum pay, social insurance, and support for organizing.

6. Conclusion

Food delivery platforms must balance efficiency with human agency. Riders are not just data points but spatial experts. Incorporating their voice can help build a fairer, more sustainable platform ecosystem.
